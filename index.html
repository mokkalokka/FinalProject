The Problem

Autistic Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is estimated to affect about 1 percent of the worlds population. One of the more common traits of the condition is certain difficulties associated with social and emotional skills.

These difficulties have great impact on people with ASD's social relations, which again affects their education, work prospects and indeed most of their lives.

Early diagnosis of the disorder is important, because therapy has been shown to have the largest impact for the individual in developing years. An intervention at this point can help reduce difficult behaviour, thus making it easier to socialise.

We at socible believe that by dealing with these problems, our society can more fully realize ASD individuals unique strengths.

It is important to note that ASD is a spectrum with a wide range of severity and with different characteristics, but they all tend to share these difficulties with social and emotional skills.

Social education through VR-technology

Our approach for dealing with the problem consists of innovative intervention using virtual reality (VR). A virtual simulation allows individuals to practice different social scenarios.

Virtual Reality is already showing itself to be a promising educational resource for individuals with ASD. In fact, multiple projects are currently utilizing VR precisely for this effort.

Unfortunately these initiatives utilize expensive equipment and software. As such, they are only available to a privileged few.

This begs the question. How do we make this approach accessible for everyone?

The Product

The solution is simple. It has already been demonstrated that smartphones together with cheap cardboard can produce satisfying VR experiences.

We take advantage of this with a simple app. Socible is a VR simulation game designed for smartphones.

It consists of different simulations with two key focal points:

  • Exposing the user to particular social situations to gain social competence.
  • Teaching the user to identify facial expressions and body language in relation to correlating emotions.

The app can be used as is, or more functionality can be added with a bluetooth controller – another cheap and available option.

Our goal is for socible to be available for everyone, in any language. That's why we're making it an open source project. With continuous contributions the project can grow a multiple of exciting ways.

Design

The design of this website is intended as a demonstration of the general UI principles of socible.

We aim for a universally designed product that works for everyone, on older as well as new and more advanced smartphones.

As such, this website has been developed and tested for most all browsers. It is also designed to be easy and fast to run, even on slower hardware.

In addition to following best practice and WCAG guidelines, special consideration will be given to catering the experience for users with ASD. This involves:

  • Having a clean and uncluttered design.
  • Avoid use of bright colors, use soft, mild colors.
  • Avoiding animations and movement that is not mutable.
  • Consistency in the UI.
  • Simple and direct language - avoiding ambiguous terms and metaphors.

Alert readers will recognize that some of these guidelines conflict with typical design choices in products made for children – for instance bright colors and sharp contrasts. By consulting with individuals with ASD we have come to the conclusion to put ASD needs first.

Early intervention: Intervention for children with ASD

Numerous academic papers have proven virtual reality as a promising resource for the development of social adaptation and emotional skills.

In a study from 2018, researchers from Hong Kong conducted a trial using virtual reality as a teaching method for children with ASD. They found significant improvements in the subjects social abilities and emotional expression.

VR therapy has been shown to have a positive impact on young adults as well. This is the subject of a study from 2012 Virtual Reality Social Cognition Training for Young Adults with High-Functioning Autism

On UI development for individuals with ASD: User Interface for People with Autism Spectrum Disorders, Professor Nikolay Pavlov, Plovdiv University.
