The Problem

People with Autistic Spectrum Disorder (ASD) has certain difficulties associated with social and emotional skills. These problems has “can” a negative impact on social relations, education, work and society as a whole. The main reason is difficulties in recognising and understanding various elements in social interactions. Some of these elements are facial expressions, body language and social situations.

An early diagnose is important because therapy has been shown to have the biggest positive impact for the individual in the early years raisingchildren.net. Children with ASD will often, among other things, go into tantrums because it’s difficult to express their needs to their peers. An early intervention will help the children to better express their needs and can help reduce difficult behaviour, thus making it easier to socialise.

It is important to note that ASD is a spectrum with a wide range of severity and with different characteristics, but they all tend to share these difficulties with social and emotional skills.

The Solution

Our product is an affordable and innovative intervention for children with ASD to practice different social scenarios using virtual reality (VR) simulation. The two main strategies of the simulation is:

  • To expose the user to a particular social situation to gain social competence.
  • To identify facial expressions and body language in relation to the correlating emotion.

Our product does also take into account that the users might not have the resources to be able to visit a specialist on the subject. Our solution to this aspect is to use a smartphone that is running the simulation and a cardboard VR mount for the phone. This will make it highly accessible for most people. The simulations will be developed open source and encourage other developers to collaborate and create an ongoing better solution.

Research

Numerous academic papers have proven virtual reality as a promising resource for the development of social adaptation and emotional skills.

In a study from 2018, researchers from Hong Kong conducted a trial using virtual reality as a teaching method for children with ASD. They found significant improvements in the subjects social abilities and emotional expression.

Even though early intervention is important, VR therapy has been shown to have a positive impact on young adults as well. This is the subject of a study from 2012 Virtual Reality Social Cognition Training for Young Adults with High-Functioning Autism

Design

The design of this website is intended as a demonstration of the general UI principles of the "produktnavnher".

Our goal is for "produktnavn" to be a universally designed product that works for everyone and on a multitude of platforms. In addition to following best practice and WCAG guidelines, special consideration will also be given to catering the experience for users with ASD. This involves:

  • Having a clean and uncluttered design.
  • Avoiding animations and movement that is not mutable.
  • Consistency in the UI.
  • Simple and direct language - avoiding ambiguous terms and metaphors.
